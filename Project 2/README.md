# Project 2: Fully Mechanical Vehicle

## Project Overview

This project focused on the design, development, and testing of a fully mechanical vehicle powered by a bungee cord energy storage system. The objective was to maximize vehicle performance while maintaining a lightweight, cost-effective, and reliable design.

Throughout the project, CAD modeling and engineering analysis were used to design individual components and integrate them into a complete vehicle assembly. To reduce overall weight and improve efficiency, unnecessary material was removed from the frame while maintaining structural integrity. Cost considerations also played a significant role in the design process, leading the team to eliminate bearings and instead develop alternative solutions that maintained functionality while reducing complexity and manufacturing costs.

A continuously variable transmission (CVT) mechanism was incorporated to improve the winding process of the bungee cord and increase energy storage efficiency. Additionally, pulleys were integrated directly into the frame to guide the bungee cord and reduce friction during the unwinding process, allowing for smoother energy transfer to the drivetrain. Multiple design iterations, testing sessions, and performance evaluations were conducted to optimize vehicle speed, reliability, and overall effectiveness.

The project provided experience in mechanical system design, energy storage mechanisms, lightweight structural design, drivetrain development, CAD modeling, prototyping, and engineering problem-solving while demonstrating the complete engineering design process from concept development through final testing.
